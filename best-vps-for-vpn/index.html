What is a VPN?

A Virtual Private Network, VPN for short, ensures online privacy by hiding your IP address. It acts as a secure bridge to the internet, assigning you a different IP when connected to a private server. Encryption transforms your internet data, making it unreadable to hackers and even your ISP. This encrypted connection forms a secure 'tunnel' for online activities. Additionally, a VPN can change your virtual location, maintaining privacy and granting access to region-specific content, like shows available only in certain countries on streaming platforms.

A VPS VPN server is a VPN hosted on a Virtual Private Server, also known as VPN with VPS. This solution is an affordable, secure, and highly private way of working with VPNs as you never have to worry about your sensitive data being shared with a VPN provider.

Making Your Own Self-Hosted VPN

While there are a number of excellent VPN providers out there that you can use for your needs, but as I mentioned before, it is worthwhile to consider the possibility of hosting your own VPN. Of course, it will take more time to set it up, and since you're the creator, the maintenance and taking care of this server is up to you. Among the advantages are the considerably faster speed and lower latency if you host your VPN with VPS. You also don't have to worry about any hardware failures. You'll be in full control of your data, it'll be cheaper for you, and you can use your VPN for more than just security. Also, websites that ban VPN usage probably won't detect a self-hosted VPN.

Choosing the Best VPS for VPN Factors to Consider

The rise of VPS, Virtual Private Server, and its many advantages have made it a readily available tool that many users employ to host their own VPN with VPS. Choosing the best VPS VPN server depends on several factors. These factors will help you understand what characteristics the best VPS for VPN has and decide which provider best offers the services you need.

Cost

One of the most essential factors to consider is cost. A valid reason why we choose self-hosted VPNs is that they are much cheaper than purchasing a VPN from a provider. Considering that, when youre looking to buy VPS for VPN, you need to find an affordable provider. VPS providers offer different plans with various specs. Depending on the hardware resources you need, choose a provider that can offer you optimized and cheap VPS for VPN hosting that wont break the bank.

Bandwidth

Scalability is a huge aspect of finding a good VPS provider. But the scalability we need to consider for the best VPS for VPN is network bandwidth. VPS providers that offer VPN hosting servers must offer high or unlimited bandwidth, with the opportunity to add more bandwidth whenever needed. Your VPN connection speed and the number of users depend on your VPN servers bandwidth.

Server Location

Your VPS VPN server location primarily depends on why you need a VPN. For example, if you need a VPN for unblocking streaming services, a VPS location in the States will be suitable. If you need a VPN for a fast connection while online gaming, you need a VPS server close to your games server. To find the best VPS for VPN server, go for a provider that offers a wide range of diverse locations.

Independence

Next, you want to make sure that the VPS service you're going to use as a host for your VPN is not in any way compromised or watched. Even if the VPN itself is very secure, it is ultimately hosted on another computer, so check out the certificates and accolades of your candidates.

Uptime

Lastly, you'll want to make sure that your chosen VPS host has a low maintenance time. All VPS hosts have to shut down the server from time to time to run maintenance on their hardware. While this downtime is inevitable, it's best to look for a candidate that has managed its downtime to the bare minimum. Look for uptime of 99% and higher.

Operating System

You can host your VPN server on both Windows and Linux distributions. Since hosting a VPN on a Windows server is more complex, VPS for VPN providers usually only offer Linux distributions. Go for a provider that offers CentOS, Ubuntu, or Debian.

FAQ

What is the Difference Between VPS vs. VPN?

A VPS is a hosting web service that allows you to use another computer in another location, usually for its location or better quality, in exchange for money. A VPN is an online tunnel connection that masks your IP address and encrypts your data in order to give you privacy and anonymity.

Is a Self-Hosted VPN better than Buying a VPN from a Provider?

It depends. Doing so costs less and gives you better customization changes, better control of your data, and lets you use websites that ban VPNs. However, encryption and anonymity become harder to get, and you won't have as much control over your VPN location.

Can I Host My Own VPN?

Yes. There are several different methods to do this, but the most reliable is hosting it on another computer via using a VPS provider. We listed ten of the best options above, including Cloudzy itself!
